## English

Spatial Design Mentor.skills is an early-stage architecture and spatial design assistant for students, designers, and educators.

It helps users move from site evidence to design thinking by reviewing project briefs, identifying missing information, reading site conditions, matching relevant architect methods, and supporting design critique and presentation structure.

The current version focuses on the design mentoring framework:

- Project brief review
- Site and context analysis guidance
- Architect and design method matching
- Concept and strategy development
- Critique support
- Presentation and jury narrative support

Spatial Design Mentor.skills is not intended to generate finished designs or imitate famous architects. It is intended to help users ask better design questions, build stronger spatial reasoning, and explain design decisions with evidence.

## How to Use

Use Spatial Design Mentor as a studio mentor during early or mid-stage design development.

Provide as much project information as possible:

- Project type: museum, housing, library, community center, school, park, renovation, pavilion, etc.
- Site condition: slope, river, old town, campus, dense urban fabric, rural landscape, waterfront, existing building, etc.
- Users and program: who uses the project, what spaces are required, and how they operate.
- Design intention: atmosphere, publicness, quietness, flexibility, memory, ecological repair, structural clarity, etc.
- Current material: brief, site description, plan, section, sketch, model photo, precedent references, or draft concept.

Recommended prompt format:

```text
Project type:
Site condition:
Users and program:
Current design idea:
Main problem I am struggling with:
What I need: site analysis / architect method matching / critique / presentation structure
```

Expected response:

- Input audit
- Key site readings
- Main design problem
- Recommended architect methods
- Design strategies
- Next drawings to produce
- Critique risks
- Presentation narrative

## Examples

### Example 1: Mountain Museum

Input:

```text
I am designing a small mountain museum. The north side connects to an urban road, the south side faces a river, and the site drops about 8 meters from road to river. I want the building to feel quiet and partly embedded in the terrain, but it still needs a clear public entrance.
```

Possible guidance:

- Main problem: connect city arrival to river landscape through section.
- Method references: Tadao Ando for path, wall, and light; Peter Zumthor for atmosphere and material; Snohetta for public ground; Alvaro Siza for site repair.
- Next drawings: road-to-river section, entrance height-difference studies, noise/quiet zoning, gallery sequence diagram.

### Example 2: Old Town Community Center

Input:

```text
I am designing a community center in an old town. The site is surrounded by narrow streets, small shops, and old residential buildings. Users include elderly residents, children, and local cultural groups. I want it to feel open and public, but I worry about noise, privacy, and heritage context.
```

Possible guidance:

- Main problem: create a public neighborhood room while protecting quiet and age-specific spaces.
- Method references: Lina Bo Bardi for collective public culture; Alvaro Siza for urban edge repair; Wang Shu for local memory; Zhang Ke for micro-renewal.
- Next drawings: public/private gradient, street-to-courtyard section, activity schedule overlay, keep/remove/add diagram.

### Example 3: Campus Library

Input:

```text
I am designing a campus library between dormitories and teaching buildings. Students need quiet reading, group work, lectures, cafe, and 24-hour study. I want the library to become the heart of the campus, not just a storage building for books.
```

Possible guidance:

- Main problem: make the library a campus commons while preserving gradients of noise, access, daylight, and study intensity.
- Method references: Louis Kahn for room character and served/servant spaces; Alvar Aalto for humane learning environments; SANAA for soft boundaries; OMA for program as social script.
- Next drawings: campus movement diagram, noise gradient, day/night access diagram, section through commons, stacks, reading, and 24-hour zone.

## 中文

Spatial Design Mentor.skills 是一个面向建筑、空间设计、景观、城市设计学生和设计者的建筑大师设计导师技能。

它帮助使用者从场地证据出发，逐步完成任务书审查、信息缺失判断、场地条件分析、建筑师方法匹配、设计批评和汇报逻辑整理。

当前版本主要关注设计导师框架：

- 项目任务书审查
- 场地与周边环境分析引导
- 建筑师与设计方法匹配
- 概念与策略推导
- 设计评图与修改建议
- 汇报、答辩和作品集叙事支持

Spatial Design Mentor.skills 不是为了直接生成最终设计，也不是为了模仿某位著名建筑师的风格。它的目标是帮助使用者提出更好的设计问题，建立更清晰的空间推理，并用场地和功能证据解释设计决策。

## 使用指南

可以把 Spatial Design Mentor 当作早期或中期设计阶段的工作室导师使用。

建议提供尽量完整的项目信息：

- 项目类型：美术馆、住宅、图书馆、社区中心、学校、公园、改造、展亭等
- 场地条件：坡地、河流、旧城、校园、高密度城市肌理、乡村景观、滨水、既有建筑等
- 使用者与功能：谁使用这个项目，需要哪些空间，如何运营
- 设计意图：氛围、公共性、安静、灵活性、记忆、生态修复、结构清晰性等
- 当前材料：任务书、场地描述、平面、剖面、草图、模型照片、案例参考或概念草案

推荐输入格式：

```text
项目类型：
场地条件：
使用者与功能：
当前设计想法：
目前卡住的问题：
我需要：场地分析 / 建筑师方法匹配 / 评图 / 汇报结构
```

预期输出：

- 输入内容审核
- 关键场地判断
- 主要设计问题
- 推荐建筑师方法
- 设计策略
- 下一步需要画的图
- 评图风险
- 汇报叙事

## 使用案例

### 案例 1：山地美术馆

输入：

```text
我在设计一个小型山地美术馆。北侧连接城市道路，南侧面向河流，场地从道路到河流大约下降 8 米。我希望建筑安静，并且部分嵌入地形，但仍然需要清晰的公共入口。
```

可能得到的指导：

- 主要问题：通过剖面把城市入口和河岸景观连接起来。
- 方法参考：安藤忠雄用于路径、墙与光；彼得·卒姆托用于氛围和材料；Snohetta 用于公共地面；西扎用于场地修复。
- 下一步图纸：道路到河流的剖面、入口高差方案、噪声/安静分区、展厅序列图。

### 案例 2：旧城社区中心

输入：

```text
我在旧城里设计一个社区中心。基地周围是窄街、小商铺和老住宅。使用者包括老人、儿童和地方文化团体。我希望它开放而公共，但担心噪声、隐私和历史文脉的问题。
```

可能得到的指导：

- 主要问题：创造一个面向社区的公共房间，同时保护安静空间和不同年龄人群的使用边界。
- 方法参考：Lina Bo Bardi 用于集体公共文化；西扎用于城市边界修复；王澍用于地方记忆；张轲用于微更新。
- 下一步图纸：公共/私密梯度、街道到庭院剖面、活动时间表叠图、保留/拆除/新增图。

### 案例 3：校园图书馆

输入：

```text
我在宿舍和教学楼之间设计一个校园图书馆。学生需要安静阅读、小组讨论、讲座、咖啡和 24 小时自习。我希望图书馆成为校园中心，而不是单纯的藏书建筑。
```

可能得到的指导：

- 主要问题：让图书馆成为校园公共客厅，同时处理噪声、出入、日照和学习强度的梯度。
- 方法参考：路易·康用于房间性格和服务/被服务空间；阿尔瓦·阿尔托用于人性化学习环境；SANAA 用于柔性边界；OMA 用于功能作为社会剧本。
- 下一步图纸：校园动线图、噪声梯度、日夜出入图、穿过公共空间、书库、阅读区和 24 小时区域的剖面。
